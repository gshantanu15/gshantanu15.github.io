Full-Wave Bridge Rectifier

As a part of my Physics Project, I built a Full-Wave rectifier that converts Alternating Current to Direct Current, using a PCB Board, diodes, a capacitor, a transformer, switch, terminals, an LED and connecting wires. The rectifier was also tested in the Physics Lab using the in-lab oscilloscope, and the effect of the capcitor can be seen as it removes the ripples in output waveform. The hands-on approach was a great way to learn concepts.

Raspberry Pi Robotic Car

I built a Python programmed Robotic Car using a Raspberry Pi 3. I used a toy car's housing to house the electronic components, like the Pi itself, a battery, a breadboard, sensors and the motors. The car does not need an active connection to a wall socket, since it is made to be battery-powered (battery also powers the Pi). It also buzzes when the sensors detect an obsticle. It was fun working on this during my summer break.
